Master's Research Project

AURIGA: Robotic
Scrub Nurse

Addressing the healthcare professional shortage by bridging the gap between "black box" robotic policies and supervisable clinical assistance using Imitation Learning and Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models.

01
Hardware & Telemanipulation

Bimanual Data Collection Platform

Implementation of a leader-follower telemanipulation system using the Dobot X-Trainer to gather high-quality human demonstrations from actual surgical nurses.

Configured a 14 Degrees of Freedom (DoF) bimanual platform.
Multimodal data collection: Joint angles and 3x RGB-Depth camera feeds.
Gathered ~50 clinical demonstrations per specific surgical task.
Dobot X-Trainer Telemanipulation Multimodal Data
02
Core Architecture

Language-Guided Imitation Learning

Designed a novel architecture integrating textual input to improve classical imitation learning. This enables explicit task validation and prevents the robot from blindly executing erroneous maneuvers.

Task decomposition using LLaMA-3-instruct LLMs.
Closed-loop Visual Question Answering (VQA) to confirm task success.
Implementation and benchmarking of SoTA policies like ALOHA (ACT) and OpenVLA.
LLaMA-3 OpenVLA VQA ALOHA ACT
03
Clinical Tasks & Evaluation

Executing Surgical Maneuvers

Evaluated the trained policies in physical mock-up OR environments. The robot successfully executes complex actions requiring high precision and bi-manual coordination.

Instrument Handover: Grasping, orienting, and transferring surgical tools safely.
Peel Pack Opening: Bimanual coordination to grasp tabs and pull sterile bags open.
Spatial Generalization: Tested across variable random/static orientations and translations.
Bimanual Coordination Spatial Generalization Clinical Evaluation
